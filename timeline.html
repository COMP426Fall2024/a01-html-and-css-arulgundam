Timeline of Karna's Life

Era Events
Birth and Early Life Divine Birth: Born to Kunti and Surya, Karna is blessed with divine armor (Kavacha) and earrings (Kundala). Kunti, unmarried at the time, abandons him in a basket in the river to avoid societal disgrace.
Adoption by Adhiratha and Radha: The basket is found by Adhiratha, a charioteer, and his wife Radha, who raise Karna as their own son. Karna grows up believing himself to be a charioteer's son (Suta Putra).
Youth and Training Training Under Drona: Karna approaches Dronacharya but is rejected due to his low-born status.
Training Under Parashurama: Karna lies about his caste to train under Parashurama, becoming an exceptional warrior. He is cursed by Parashurama to forget his skills at his greatest need.
Curse by a Brahmin: Karna accidentally kills a Brahmin's cow while practicing archery, resulting in a curse that his chariot wheel will get stuck during a crucial battle.
Public Challenge and Humiliation: Karna challenges Arjuna at an archery tournament but is mocked and not allowed to participate.
Karna’s Role in the Kuru Court Friendship with Duryodhana: Duryodhana makes Karna the king of Anga, elevating his social status. Karna swears lifelong loyalty to Duryodhana.
Insulting Draupadi: During the dice game, Karna humiliates Draupadi by calling her a whore and asking her to undress in front of the court, deepening the animosity between him and the Pandavas.
Life as a Warrior and Conflicts Marriage and Family: Karna marries Vrushali (or Padmavati) and has several sons, including Vrishasena and Banasena, who fight in the Kurukshetra war.
Karna's Rivalry with Arjuna: Karna and Arjuna have a fierce rivalry, with Karna motivated to prove his superiority over Arjuna.
Crowning of Yudhishthira: Karna opposes the Pandavas during Yudhishthira’s Rajasuya Yagna but plays a lesser role compared to Duryodhana.
Public Challenge and Humiliation: Karna publicly challenges Arjuna at the archery tournament held by Dronacharya but is mocked for his low birth and not allowed to participate.
War and Revelation of His Heritage Karna Learns of His True Parentage: Kunti reveals to Karna that he is her son and the eldest of the Pandavas. Karna refuses to join his brothers, remaining loyal to Duryodhana and keeping his parentage a secret.
Commander of Kaurava Army: After Bhishma and Drona fall, Karna becomes the commander of the Kaurava army.
Final Duel with Arjuna: On the 17th day of the war, Karna's chariot wheel gets stuck due to the Brahmin's curse. While he is distracted, Arjuna kills him with an arrow at Krishna’s urging.
Posthumous Events Karna’s Identity Revealed: After Karna’s death, Kunti reveals to the Pandavas that Karna was their elder brother, leaving them devastated. Yudhishthira is heartbroken, and in some versions, he curses all women never to be able to keep secrets.
